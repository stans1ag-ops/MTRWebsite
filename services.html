Orthopedic Rehab

Orthopedic rehab helps people recover from injuries or pain in the muscles, joints, bones, ligaments, or tendons. This may include problems with the back, neck, shoulder, hip, knee, ankle, or other areas of the body. Your therapist works with you to reduce pain, improve movement, rebuild strength, and help you return to everyday activities with more comfort and confidence.

Sports Rehab

Sports rehab helps athletes and active people recover from injuries and safely return to the activities they enjoy. This service can help with sprains, strains, overuse injuries, weakness, pain, or movement problems. Your therapist will guide you through exercises and treatment that match your sport, your goals, and your current stage of recovery.

Post-Surgical Care

Post-surgical care helps you heal and regain strength after surgery. Your therapist will follow your doctor's instructions and guide you through safe movement, gentle exercises, and gradual strengthening. The goal is to help reduce pain and stiffness, protect the surgical area, and help you get back to daily activities as safely as possible.

Lymphedema

Lymphedema is swelling that can happen when fluid builds up in part of the body, often in an arm or leg. Therapy can help manage this swelling, improve comfort, and teach you ways to care for the area at home. Treatment may include gentle hands-on care, wrapping or compression, exercise, and education to help reduce symptoms and support daily life.

Neurologic Rehab

Neurologic rehab helps people who have movement, balance, strength, or coordination problems related to the nervous system. This may include recovery after a stroke, brain injury, spinal cord injury, or other neurologic condition. Your therapist works with you on safe exercises and daily movement skills to help improve independence and confidence.

LSVT Parkinson's

LSVT Parkinson's therapy is designed for people living with Parkinson's disease who want to improve movement in daily life. It focuses on practicing bigger, more confident movements to help with walking, balance, posture, and everyday tasks. Your therapist will guide you through structured exercises and help you carry those movements into normal routines.

Blood Flow Restriction

Blood flow restriction is a treatment that uses a special cuff during exercise to help muscles work harder while using lighter weights or resistance. This can be helpful when heavy strengthening is not comfortable or not yet safe. Your therapist controls the cuff and exercise plan so the work is matched to your needs and recovery goals.

Dry Needling

Dry needling is a treatment that uses very thin needles to help reduce muscle tightness and pain. The needle is placed into tight or tender spots in the muscle to help the area relax and move better. It is often used along with exercise, stretching, and other therapy so you can make progress with less discomfort.

Aquatic Therapy

Aquatic therapy is physical therapy done in water. The water helps support your body, which can make movement feel easier and reduce stress on sore joints. It can be helpful for pain, weakness, balance concerns, or recovery after injury or surgery. Your therapist uses the water to help you move, strengthen, and build confidence in a low-impact setting.

Vestibular Therapy

Vestibular therapy helps people who have dizziness, vertigo, balance problems, or unsteadiness. Your therapist will look at what triggers your symptoms and guide you through exercises that help your body and brain respond better to movement. The goal is to reduce dizziness, improve balance, and help you feel safer during everyday activities.

Pediatric Rehab

Pediatric physical therapy is specifically designed to help children and adolescents overcome physical challenges and reach their developmental milestones. Our compassionate therapists work closely with families to create engaging, play-based treatment plans that address gross motor skills, strength, balance, and coordination. Whether recovering from an injury or managing a congenital condition, we provide a safe and supportive environment for your child to thrive.
