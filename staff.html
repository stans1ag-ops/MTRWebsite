Mark Stansberry

Owner / PT, DPT, OCS, FAAOMPT

Mark is originally from Mt. Pleasant and is a graduate of Sacred Heart Academy. He earned his Bachelor's degree in Health Science from the University of Michigan-Flint and completed his Master's in Physical Therapy at U of M-Flint in 1992. He earned his doctorate in physical therapy from Rocky Mountain University in Provo, Utah. He is a graduate of the URSA Foundation under the direction of Dr. Loren Rex. He has extensive training in manual techniques and has completed numerous courses on Muscle Energy Techniques (MET) including coursework instructed by Dr. Fred Mitchell and Dr. Jay Sandweiss. Mark is certified in Intramuscular Manual Therapy through the Travell Series. He is a member of the American Physical Therapy Association (Orthopedic and Private Practice Sections), and the Michigan Physical Therapy Association. He is currently adjunct faculty at Central Michigan University in the Physical Therapy graduate program. Prior to opening his own business, he was the Director of Rehabilitation Services for Central Michigan Community Hospital.

Mark opened Mountain Town Rehab in 2001. He treats primarily orthopedic injuries, but enjoys treating an eclectic population with a variety of interventions. He is active in the community and enjoys the company of his family; wife, Sue, son and daughter-in-law, Alex and Theresa, and son Kyle.

Mark's patient-centered approach to providing care has created a dedicated clientele and developed a strong referral base. In fact, Mountain Town Rehab has received referrals from over 400 different physicians throughout Michigan and from a variety of states. Mark has selected highly qualified individuals to form his Mountain Town Rehab team. He has treated athletes at the youth, high school, collegiate, professional and Olympic levels. Mountain Town Rehab is the premiere choice for physical therapy in Central Michigan and your choice for personal, quality care.

Sue Stansberry

Owner / Office Manager

Sue is originally from Mt. Pleasant and is a graduate of Sacred Heart Academy. She earned her BS degree in Math and Accounting from Central Michigan University. Sue and her husband Mark opened Mountain Town Rehab in 2001 and she is thankful for the opportunity to work alongside her husband while serving the community they both were raised in. As practice manager and billing specialist, Sue enjoys the Mountain Town Rehab Team and the patients they serve with the goal of making everyone feel like they are at home in the clinic.

Prior to opening Mountain Town Rehab, Sue enjoyed being a stay-at-home mom. Outside of work she enjoys the many events offered by Central Michigan University, reading, and spending time with her family. She is especially fond of the time spent with her grandchildren.

Kristi Pifer

MA, MSPT

Kristi is originally from Rosebush. She received her BS degree from Michigan State University and her MS in Physical Therapy as well as an MA degree in Exercise Physiology from Central Michigan University. Kristi has worked with Mark Stansberry since 1998 when they were employed by CMCH - Wellness Central. Kristi came to Mountain Town Rehab in 2003.

Kristi received her lymphedema certification (LLCC) in 2003. While she also specializes in pediatric physical therapy and aquatic therapy, she treats a variety of orthopedic and neurologic conditions. Outside of her professional life, Kristi has four boys, which keep her busy attending sporting events, camping, and travelling. She enjoys hiking, gardening, and challenging herself with fitness goals.

Alex Stansberry

DPT

Alex is from Mount Pleasant. He earned his undergraduate degree, a B.S. in Health Fitness, from CMU in 2010. He continued his CMU education and graduated in May 2013 with a Doctorate in Physical Therapy. Alex came to Mountain Town Rehab in May 2013.

Alex has clinical experience in outpatient settings with patients of various orthopedic and neurologic conditions, as well as experience in inpatient rehabilitation with patients of various neurologic conditions. Alex is interested in working with patients who have a broad range of orthopedic conditions, with more specific interests in working with individuals who have sports related injuries. He is certified in Intramuscular Manual Therapy (IMT) and Blood Flow Restriction Training (BFRT) and has extensive training in Graston technique.

In his personal time, Alex enjoys spending time with family and friends, as well as biking, golfing, playing volleyball, reading, and camping.
